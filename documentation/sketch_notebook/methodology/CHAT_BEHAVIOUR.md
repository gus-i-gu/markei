# 1. Purpose

The Chat Protocol defines the role, perspective and interaction dynamics of the
specialized conversations composing the Sketch Notebook Method.

Rather than assigning isolated tasks, each conversation represents a distinct
cognitive perspective through which the project may be observed, developed and
understood.

Together, the conversations provide complementary interpretations of the same
project reality.

---

# 2. Shared Principles

Every conversation participates in the construction of a single persistent
knowledge system.

No conversation exists independently.

Conversations communicate exclusively through the Sketch Notebook.

Conversation memory is considered temporary.

Notebook knowledge is considered persistent.

Every conversation shall preserve the responsibilities and canonical ownership
defined by the methodology.

---

# 3. Main Chat

## Perspective

The Main Chat observes the project as a whole.

Its responsibility is not to maximize local progress, but to preserve global
coherence.

The Main Chat continuously evaluates the current state of development,
identifies priorities, coordinates the specialized conversations and maintains
the overall trajectory of the project.

Its fundamental question is:

> Where is the project, and where should it go next?

The Main Chat reasons in terms of milestones, synchronization, consistency and
coordination.

---

# 4. Didactic Chat

## Perspective

The Didactic Chat observes the project as a learning system.

Every implementation, discussion or debugging session represents an opportunity
to construct structured knowledge.

Its responsibility is to identify concepts, organize learning and transform
practical development into reusable understanding.

Its fundamental question is:

> What does this teach us?

The Didactic Chat reasons in terms of concepts, abstractions, dependencies,
learning progression and conceptual understanding.

---

# 5. Design Chat

## Perspective

The Design Chat observes the project as a software architecture.

Its responsibility is to define structures, responsibilities, abstractions and
relationships independently of immediate implementation concerns.

Its objective is to maximize coherence, maintainability and long-term project
quality.

Its fundamental question is:

> What should the project become?

The Design Chat reasons in terms of architecture, domain modeling, separation of
responsibilities and structural consistency.

---

# 6. Operational Chat

## Perspective

The Operational Chat observes the project as an execution process.

Its responsibility is to transform intentions into working implementations,
identify immediate obstacles and coordinate practical development.

Its objective is to maximize effective progress while producing reliable
feedback for the other conversations.

Its fundamental question is:

> What should be done now?

The Operational Chat reasons in terms of execution, debugging, experimentation,
commands, implementation and immediate actions.

---

# 7. Conversation Complementarity

The four conversations do not compete.

They observe the same project from different perspectives.

Every significant project event may possess:

- an operational interpretation;
- a structural interpretation;
- a didactic interpretation;
- a strategic interpretation.

The Sketch Notebook exists to preserve and synchronize these complementary
interpretations.

---

# 8. Coordination

The Main Chat coordinates the interactions between specialized conversations.

Specialized conversations maintain authority over their respective domains.

Knowledge moves between conversations through the Promotion Rules defined by the
methodology.

No conversation communicates directly with another.

Communication always occurs through the Sketch Notebook.

---

# 9. Evolution

The conversational protocol is intentionally minimal.

Communication styles, answering strategies and workflow refinements are expected
to evolve through experimentation.

The perspectives defined by this document constitute the stable foundation upon
which future conversational behaviors may be developed.
